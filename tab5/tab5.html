VIBCON

Apr 20, 2020   to   Apr 21, 2020

        The last few years have seen the emergence and growth of undergraduate medical conferences in several medical colleges across the country. What was once thought to be an event for consultants and PG students, has now gained immense popularity among students of MBBS.

 

        Medical conferences allow undergraduates to go a step further from their routine academics and get a glimpse of the practical applications and implications of what they learn in books. Workshops, keynote speeches, quiz, symposium, case and paper presentation are the events that are generally present at UG medical conferences. Workshops and drills equip students with hands-on experience in essential clinical skills whereas research presentation opens their minds to question already acquired knowledge. Events such as the medical symposium give students a unique platform to integrate research, problem solving and creativity. All in all, these events help broaden the students' horizons and give them a fresh perspective on medicine.

 

        Keeping this in mind, we, a team of students from Aakriti Chopra Batch (second first MBBS), would like to organise a 2 day long medical conference for undergraduate students at our college. Having participated in such conferences ourselves, we would like the students of our college to get an exposure to events of this kind and make our college a hub of such academic activities.

  • The conference will be open to all undergraduate MBBS students.
  • The dates will be decided keeping in mind the exams of all the batches.
  • Faculty from all departments will be involved in this event. A committee will be formed comprising of one faculty from each department.
  • Judges for different events will be chosen from the faculty on the advice of the aforementioned committee.
  • Finances will be covered from the registration fees and sponsorship from willing companies.

Events

Workshops

Workshops will be aimed at engaging students in interactive learning sessions; providing the most up-to-date information and strategies; and developing or enhancing skills that they may apply in clinical practice, research, or teaching.

  • Medical emergencies, ECG Interpretation and red flag signs
  • Basic Surgical Skills
  • Basics of oncology- examination of common malignancies
  • basics of radiology

Keynote Speakers/CMEs

Eminent doctors will be invited to share their knowledge, experiences and new developments in the field of medicine.

Quiz

From the pages of the textbooks to their hands-on, real world implications, the quiz will test the medical studentâ€™s clinical quotient in all 19 subjects of medicine.

 

This will be a team event, with each team comprising of 3 participants.

 

There will be a preliminary round, followed by a final round.

Paper Presentation

This event gives undergraduate students the opportunity to present compelling research on topics concerning the medical sciences, helping them to present their own unique view in this ever-growing field of medical exploration.

 

Researches of many students of our college have been selected by ICMR for the Short Term Studentship program. This will be a platform for these students to present their work to an audience.

 

The papers will be judged by the faculty of our college.

Case Presentation

This event gives students the opportunity to present a plethora of intriguing cases and showcase their clinical acumen.

 

The presentation should comprise only of the relevant positive and negative findings in history, vital parameters on examination, general and systemic examination findings, differential diagnoses proposed, laboratory/special investigations, final diagnosis, therapeutic /surgical interventions made, their outcomes and finally a summary of all the above.

 

The cases will be judged by the faculty of our college

Medical Symposium

The Medical Symposium is the culmination of inquisitive thinking and flawless presentation. It is an event wherein a team of undergraduate students from each college presents a medically relevant study with a fresh perspective after thorough research.

 

Each team will comprise of 12 students. Each team will have not more than 6 speakers.

 

The presentation will be of max. 20 minutes.
